The term "intellectual property" began to be used in the 19th century, though it was not until the late 20th century that intellectual property became commonplace in most of the world's legal systems. IPR stands for "Intellectual Property Rights."

Intellectual property refers to creations of the mind, such as inventions, literary and artistic works, symbols, names, and images used in commerce. Intellectual property rights are legal rights that protect the intellectual property of individuals and organizations, granting them exclusive rights and control over their creations.

Intellectual property rights include patents, copyright, industrial design rights, trademarks, plant variety rights, trade dress, geographical indications, and, in some jurisdictions, trade secrets.
